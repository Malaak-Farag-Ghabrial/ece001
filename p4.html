Automatic emergency braking (AEB) on vehicles an impending collsion is detected, a warning is generated for the driver. If the collision becomes imminent, the system can apply the brakes without any driver the imput. AEB can avoid vehicle collisions in March 2016 the national highway traffic safety administration announces that AEB is required on nearly all new cars sold in the united states by 2022
E-mail filters Google uses Al to ensure that the e-mail showing up in users' inbox- es is authentic. The filters can sort e-mails into the following six cate- gories-primary, social, promo- tions, updates, forums, and spam The filters help organise email so that users can find their important messages quicker also Google claims that AI filtering prevents more than 99% of spam from getting into users' inboxes
Intelligent cruise control This technology uses radar and a camera to adjust a car's speed automatically to maintain a preset distance from the vehicle ahead. feature, already on many new cars in 2019, allows the driver to relax, especially during a long trip.
Navigation assistance Google Maps can calculate the traffic time for various routes and Suggest the quickest one to a destination given the real-time traffic. can take a detour to avoid getting stuck in slow or standstill traffic due to accidents or construction.
Suicide/Self-harm prevention In 2017, Facebook launched a proactive detection feature that scans posts to detect patterns that may indicate if a user may be considering self-harm. Facebook supports this Al-powered program with human resources such as trained moderators, partnerships with local mental health organizations, and local first- responders when appropriate. Detecting suicidal thinking patterns, the Al-powered program sends mental health resources to the person and, sometimes, also to friends. In one case, the local police were notified and able to locate the person, rush him/her to the hospital, and save his/her life.
